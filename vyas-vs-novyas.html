A student at Kota usually attends classes for about 4-6 hours a day. What about the remaining hours? It is this time outside the classroom that differentiates a top ranker from an average student. As the concept of Vyas emerged and we tried to answer the longstanding problems faced by students and parents, this question clicked our minds for more than a hundred times –

                                  “How are our students and their curriculum going to be different and better than others?”

Success and failure are the final fruits of the student’s journey, the roots of which are critical thinking and academic habits. At Vyas, we observe these roots carefully for our students. It is our job to enhance the good habits and to encourage the unproductive ones. In short, we believing in doing everything that you need to succeed.

We have done the behavioural analysis of thousands of students and then built this model through which we can change their behaviour and hence improve their chances to learn, grow and succeed.

                                  “How is our student and his/her curriculum going to be different and better than others?”

Behavioural Analysis of Non-Vyas Student Behavioural Analysis of Vyas Student
Waking up at any time, no proper routine. Waking up at a fixed time, a proper routine.
Mostly no breakfast, or just ‘chai and poha’ at stalls.A healthy nutritious breakfast provided at our own mess.
Focus only on core subjects, less focus on other subjects for board exams. Focus on all the subjects as per the needs and dynamic requirements of students.
Students going to canteens or ordering food from Zomato or Swiggy for lunch and dinner. Healthy and nutritious meals provided at our own mess only.
Students have the liberty of going to doubt faculty as per their need. Hence, most of them don’t avail this facility and never turn up to the doubt faculty. There are special sessions by doubt faculty to ensure that all the doubts of students are cleared. Faculty reaches students individually to understand their concerns.
After coaching, students go to cyber cafes, game parlours or to the rooms of other students to enjoy or to party, hence losing their time & focus. There is no room of such distractions on our campus. After classes, students simply go to their own rooms and study or play in the playground for physical fitness.
Students keep high-quality smartphones and often waste their time on social media. Not just time, it also affects the health of students and their sleeping pattern. Students are allowed to keep only basic mobile phones. We want students to be focused and sincere with respect to both their careers and parents.
